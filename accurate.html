BeatSense: Python-Based Arrhythmia Detection

A system for ECG signal processing to detect and classify cardiac arrhythmias using DSP and machine learning.

The Problem

Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death globally. Traditional ECG interpretation is slow and manual, making timely diagnosis of arrhythmias challenging, especially in resource-constrained settings.

Our Solution

BeatSense employs Python-based digital signal processing and machine learning to automate ECG segmentation and classification, accurately detecting arrhythmias at scale and helping clinicians save more lives.

Problem Statement

The manual interpretation of ECG signals is time-consuming, subjective, and requires specialized expertise, leading to potential delays in diagnosing life-threatening arrhythmias. There is a need for an automated, reliable system to accelerate this process and improve patient outcomes.

Project Objectives

  • To develop a Python-based system for ECG signal acquisition and preprocessing.
  • To implement robust algorithms for R-peak detection and feature extraction.
  • To design and apply a rule-based classification system for identifying various cardiac arrhythmias.
  • To create an intuitive interface for visualizing signals and analysis results.

Our Methodology

Our system follows a precise flowchart to analyze and classify ECG signals based on established clinical rules.

1

Signal Acquisition & Preprocessing

Acquire the raw ECG signal input and apply digital noise filtering to prepare it for analysis.

2

Feature Extraction (R-Peak)

Perform R-peak detection and signal segmentation to isolate individual heartbeats and calculate RR intervals.

3

Rhythm Analysis

First-level check: The system analyzes RR intervals to determine if the heart rhythm is 'Regular' or 'Irregular'.

4

Heart Rate Classification

Second-level check: Determine heart rate to classify as Normal (60-100 bpm), Bradycardia (< 60 bpm), or Tachycardia (> 100 bpm).

5

Tachycardia Classification

If Tachycardia (> 100 bpm) is detected, it is further classified into types like Atrial Fibrillation, Atrial Flutter, Ventricular Tachycardia, Ventricular Fibrillation, and Supraventricular Tachycardia.

6

System Output & Recommendation

Generate a final analysis (e.g., 'Normal Sinus Rhythm') and a clear recommendation, such as 'Referral to Cardiologist' for detected arrhythmias.
